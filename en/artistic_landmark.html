Artistic Landmark

Artlane
🎨 Sai Ying Pun Graffiti Art District

Sai Ying Pun's graffiti spans across over a dozen buildings, forming a concentrated art area. Among them, "ARTLANE" is a graffiti district composed of Centre Street, Ki Ling Lane, and Shek Tong Tsui, gathering works by local and international artists.

"The vibrant colors make it a popular photo spot for tourists and residents alike."

Diverse Styles: The graffiti styles here vary greatly, from colorful, playful murals to thought-provoking works reflecting social issues.

For example, one artist created a large-scale mural themed around a traditional Hong Kong herbal tea shop, cleverly blending tradition with modernity.

Dynamic Updates: Graffiti culture is highly time-sensitive. Some works are created during art festivals for a limited time and may later be covered by new graffiti, adding freshness and unpredictability to exploration.

Core Graffiti Streets and Alleys
Belcher's Street
As the main artery of Sai Ying Pun, Belcher's Street is where local life meets street art. Large murals sprawl across the walls of old tong lau buildings—depicting everything from everyday Hong Kong scenes to bold, avant-garde creations, each one telling a story of the city. Walking here, you can feel the laid-back daily rhythm of the old neighborhood while being energized by the creativity of young artists, with every step offering a vivid snapshot of urban life.
Pok Fu Lam Road
Connecting Sai Ying Pun and Pok Fu Lam, this road is like an art corridor filled with surprises. Delicate graffiti pieces hide on building facades, flyover pillars, and even corner walls along the way. There's no forced "Instagram vibe" here—instead, you stumble upon thoughtful street art creations unexpectedly, turning a commute into a mini art stroll that defies stereotypes of Hong Kong's main roads.
Centre Street
One of Hong Kong's steepest streets, Centre Street is both Sai Ying Pun's most down-to-earth living hub and a natural canvas for street art. On one side, bustling markets and old cha chaan tengs fill the air with the aroma of fish balls and wonton noodles; on the other, vibrant graffiti cascades down the steep slope, seamlessly blending everyday life with avant-garde art. Here, you can soak in the slow daily rhythm of Hong Kong locals while being blown away by the bold colors of street creators, where old and new Hong Kong collide in the most vivid way.
High Street
Iconic High Street in Sai Ying Pun is a perfect blend of retro charm and modern art. Victorian-era historic buildings wind along the sloping terrain, with vines climbing the colonnades of old tong lau buildings, and decades-old shop signs still standing at street corners. Yet, as you look up, bold and avant-garde graffiti intertwines with weathered old walls, like a conversation between time and creativity. It holds the gentle details of a vintage Hong Kong picture book while brimming with the avant-garde vibe beloved by art lovers, every corner inviting you to slow down and savor.
